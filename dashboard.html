Crack WEP
ARP Request Replay — aireplay-ng -3
Most effective WEP attack. Replays ARP packets to force the AP to generate new IVs. Requires 50,000–150,000 IVs to crack.
Fake Authentication — aireplay-ng -1
Associate with the AP without being a real client. Required before ARP replay if you have no associated client.
Fragmentation Attack — aireplay-ng -5
Obtains PRGA keystream by fragmenting a single encrypted packet. Use the keystream to forge ARP packets.
KoreK ChopChop — aireplay-ng -4
Decrypts a WEP packet without knowing the key. Slower than fragmentation but works when frag fails.
Caffe Latte — aireplay-ng -6
Clientless WEP attack — works on the client device directly without AP involvement. Client must be in probe/broadcast range.
Hirte Attack — aireplay-ng -8
Extended Caffe Latte — also works against clients not actively probing. More reliable than Caffe Latte in some scenarios.
Besside-ng — Automated WEP Cracker
Fully automated tool — scans for WEP networks and cracks them automatically. Captures to /tmp/airweb/wep.cap.
Crack WEP Key — aircrack-ng
WEP cracking is purely statistical — no wordlist needed. Requires ~50,000+ IVs (plain WEP) or ~500,000+ (WEP with SKA).

Targeted Deauth — aireplay-ng -0
Sends 802.11 deauth frames. Most effective for forcing handshake capture. Use count=0 for continuous.
Deauth/Disassoc Amok — mdk4 d
Amok mode targets all APs/clients in range. Extremely disruptive.
Auth DoS — mdk4 a
Floods AP with fake auth frames — exhausts association table. Requires BSSID.
Beacon Flood — mdk4 b obsolete
Fills scan lists with fake SSIDs. Less effective on modern devices.
WIDS/WIPS Confusion — mdk4 w obsolete
Fake management frames to confuse intrusion detection systems.
Michael Shutdown — mdk4 m TKIP only
Exploits TKIP MIC failure — forces 60s AP shutdown. Only works on WPA-TKIP, no effect on WPA2-CCMP.

Attack Explanation
ARP Replay (-3)
Captures and replays ARP requests to generate IVs. Fastest if a client is active. Needs ~50k IVs.
Chopchop (-4)
Decrypts last byte of a packet without knowing the key. Slower but works without active clients.
Fragmentation (-5)
Gets keystream from a single packet. Very effective when no clients are present on the network.
Caffe-latte (-6)
Attacks isolated clients using their cached WEP keys. Useful in public hotspots.
